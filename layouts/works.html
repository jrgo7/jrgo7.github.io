Apparent Age Estimation: Challenges and Outcomes

This is an ongoing data science project on apparent age estimation. Here, we looked into the IMDB-WIKI dataset (the largest dataset for apparent age estimation) and the Deep EXpectation (DEX) method used to finetune a VGG-16 model for the age estimation task.

We are currently exploring ways to improve the DEX method through using a mean-variance loss function instead of the original cross-entropy loss function, and further finetuning on the APPA-REAL and FairFace datasets.

An Analysis on Competitive Pokémon Teams

This was a complete data science project applied to Pokémon Showdown which involved data wrangling, exploratory data analysis, data mining, and statistical inferences, made to determine patterns in team building, metagame trends, and strategic preferences that can be observed from Pokemon Showdown's Gen 5 OU match data.

We found that teams tend to be built around the concept of controlling the weather, no matter the skill level (measured via ELO) of the players. Despite being based on an older generation of Pokémon games, the strategies of each player (the metagame) still evolves over time. This project was made for CSMODEL, a data science course in DLSU.

So Near, Yet So Far: How Violence Against Women Blocks the Philippines from True Gender Equality

Before I became an undergraduate computer science student in DLSU, I was a student in Saint Jude Cathoic School (SJCS). While there, I was able to win the Harvard Alumni for Global Women's Empowerment essay writing contest in 2022.

My piece essentially expressed concern on the ironic situation of the Philippines in terms of women empowerment: despite consistently ranking as the best country in Asia in terms of closing the gender gap, violance against women remains prevalent in our nation. I proposed that just as the colonial powers have forced their way into the country, so too did their societal norms, which have overwritten our feminist precolonial past.
