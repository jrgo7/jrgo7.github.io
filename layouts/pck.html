Profile Career Keys

SAS3000 is a course in De La Salle University meant to prepare us for our on-the-job training/practicum. It is part of the broader Student Affairs Services (SAS) series of courses. Throughout the SAS series, we took a number of tests, including the Profile Career Keys (PCK) assessment, which aimed to identify some potential career options based on our interests, values, and aptitudes. Here, I will reflect on each section of my PCK results and highlight how they relate to my career development.

My Occupational Interests

My PCK results suggest that my top occupational interests are conventional and investigative interests. The PCK report defines conventional interests as interests that follow set procedures and routines, work with data and detail, and have precise standards. Investigative interests, on the other hand, are those interests that concern ideas and thinking more than physical activity, and which concern facts and figuring out problems mentally.

I believe these reported interests align with my actual interests. For my career development, I hope to seek those jobs aligned with procedures and data, such as a career in software engineering and machine learning research.

My Work Values

When it comes to my work values, the PCK results write that my top work importance values are working conditions, support, recognition, and achievement. Working conditions essentially involve getting treated and paid well; support involves getting opportunities to be trained and upskilled; recognition involves opportunity for advancement and praise; and achievement involves using my abilities and strengths to achieve something.

My career development would benefit from knowing my work values as these serve to further filter down the extensive list of careers in the world, even when considering my occupational interests.

My Aptitudes

I am proficient in a lot of things, with my PCK career ability profiler telling me I am very skilled in arithmetic reasoning, verbal ability, clerical perception, and form perception. Arithmetic reasoning involves solving problems with math and logic; verbal ability involves good communication; clerical peception involves identifying mistakes in written material, while form peception is the same but for objecctis, pictures, and drawings.

This is comforting to hear, as this encourages me to seek out those jobs which require high proficiency in those skills. Whereas my occupational interests and work values filter down the amount of careers that may be preferable to me, my aptitudes serve to increase my opportunities and career options.

My Suggested Career Choices

The PCK results seem to use a weighted average system that takes into account my occupational interests, work values, and aptitudes from the previous sections. Reading my results, the job matches with the highest weighted average suggest a career as a budget analyst, information security analyst, or as a computer network support specialist.

These career choices are a little surprising to me because I had not really considered them at all prior to reading my PCK results. For my career development, it would do me good to explore these unexpected results further, especially while looking into my previous results.
